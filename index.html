Thesis Research

MS Thesis · Arizona State University · 2024 – 2026

Vision-Language Action Models for Safety in Robot Motion

🎓 Advisor: Prof. Nakul Gopalan 🤖 Logos Robotics Lab, ASU 🎯 Target: ICRA / CoRL

This thesis investigates how Vision-Language Action (VLA) models can be used to generate safe, collision-free motion plans for robot manipulation tasks. The core challenge is enabling a robot to interpret natural language instructions and visual observations, then produce motor actions that are both task-effective and provably safe in the environment.

Work includes implementing VLA architectures (diffusion and autoregressive), training manipulation policies from pixel observations end-to-end, deploying on a Franka Emika Panda arm with real safety interlocks, and generating robot demonstration datasets through ROS2 & MuJoCo simulation. The research contributes toward ensuring that AI-driven robots can operate reliably alongside humans.
