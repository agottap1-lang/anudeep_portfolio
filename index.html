Thesis Research

MS Thesis · Arizona State University · Approved April 2026

Vision-Language Models as Proxies for Human Judgment of Robot Motion Legibility

🎓 Advisor: Prof. Nakul Gopalan 🤖 LOGOS Robotics Lab, ASU 👥 Committee: Hani ben Amor · Kunal Garg

The problem: A robot’s motion should communicate its intent clearly to anyone watching — this property is called legibility. Evaluating it today requires human observers, which is slow and hard to scale. This thesis asks: can frontier AI models (Gemini, GPT, Claude) serve as automated proxies for that human judgment?

Contribution 1 — VLM goal-inference evaluation pipeline: Robot manipulation videos are sampled into cumulative frame prefixes at selected timepoints and fed to a VLM with a structured prompt. The model returns JSON with goal probabilities (pA / pB), a key visual cue, and a legibility flag. Accuracy is measured against ground-truth labels across 8 manipulation scenarios (left-block, right-block, top drawer, bottom drawer — legible and ambiguous variants).
Results — mean goal-inference accuracy: Gemini 3 Pro 67.7%  |  Claude Opus 4.5 64.5%  |  GPT-5.4 61.3%. All three models performed better on legible trajectories than on ambiguous ones, confirming VLMs respond to genuine motion cues.

Contribution 2 — VLM Best-of-K trajectory ranking for diffusion policies: A behavior-conditioned diffusion policy (DDPM + classifier-free guidance, λ=2.0) is trained across four behaviors — legibility, predictability, safety, grounding. At inference, K candidate trajectories are generated with different noise seeds, rendered to frames, and scored by a VLM; the highest-scoring candidate is re-executed. Legibility is quantified as a lateral commitment score L over the first 30% of the trajectory.
Results: unguided baseline L=0.919 → CFG-only L=0.951 → CFG + VLM reranking L=0.955. Best-of-K scaling (unguided): K=1 → 0.732, K=4 → 0.779, K=8 → 0.797, K=16 → 0.806 — improving monotonically but never reaching a single CFG-guided trajectory (0.834). CFG and VLM reranking are complementary: guidance shapes the candidate pool; VLM selects the best within it.

Contribution 3 — Cross-model scoring benchmark: All three VLMs scored the same K=8 candidates across all four behavior dimensions. Predictability received the highest scores (0.67–0.92); safety the lowest (0.17–0.37). The relative ranking of behaviors was consistent across model families even though absolute calibration differed — supporting VLM scoring as a stable qualitative signal.

Environment: PyBullet physics simulation, simulated Franka Panda arm, Python. Gemini 3 Pro / GPT-5.4 / Claude Opus 4.5 APIs queried via structured prompts with JSON schema enforcement.

Cooperative UGV Coverage for Disaster-Zone Cell Network Deployment

Designed and analyzed a distributed multi-robot coverage controller for deploying UGVs to restore cellular connectivity in disaster zones. Modeled the environment as a bounded planar domain and used a Voronoi-based locational cost framework where each UGV drives toward the population-weighted centroid of its Voronoi cell.
