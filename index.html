Research

I am interested in making robots more legible — easier for the people around them to read and trust. I study whether frontier vision-language models can automate the quality check that today requires human observers, and how those judgments can guide robot policy learning.

Vision-Language Models as Proxies for Human Judgment of Robot Motion Legibility
Anudeep Sai Gottapu, Nakul Gopalan
MS Thesis · LOGOS Robotics Lab, Arizona State University · Approved April 2026 · Committee: Hani ben Amor, Kunal Garg
Can AI watch a robot move and tell you if its intent is clear — the way a human observer would? We build a prefix-frame prompting pipeline and benchmark three frontier VLMs on 8 manipulation scenarios: Gemini 3 Pro (67.7%), Claude Opus 4.5 (64.5%), GPT-5.4 (61.3%). We then use VLM scores to guide Best-of-K trajectory selection inside a behavior-conditioned diffusion policy, lifting legibility from L=0.732 to L=0.955 — showing VLM scoring and CFG are complementary, not interchangeable.

LLM Alignment via Direct Preference Optimization (DPO)
Feb – Apr 2025
Preference data from humans is all you need to align a language model — no reward model required. We implemented SFT + DPO on Anthropic’s HH-RLHF dataset from scratch on A100, cutting training time 22% while boosting alignment 25.8%. The next question: can the same preference signal be applied to robot trajectory selection?

Diffusion Model Fine-Tuning with Reinforcement Learning (DDPO + LoRA)
May – Jun 2025
Every denoising step in a diffusion model is an RL action — DDPO treats it that way and fine-tunes toward any reward you can define. We applied this with LoRA to Stable Diffusion v1.5 on A100 over 100 epochs, producing 1,000+ reward-optimized outputs at ≤4 s each. The idea I want to pursue: bring reward-guided diffusion to robot motion synthesis.
arXiv — Black et al. (2023) Scholar
Motion Planning for Obstacle-Aware Autonomous Navigation
MAE 547: Robot Motion Planning · ASU · 2024
A* finds the shortest path, RRT finds any path, and IK closes the loop for a robot arm — we implemented and benchmarked all three in MATLAB on a Franka Panda with static and dynamic obstacles. Built as a foundation for mixing classical planners with learned legibility rewards to generate trajectories that are both feasible and human-interpretable.
