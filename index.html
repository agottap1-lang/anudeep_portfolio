Research

My research focuses on making robots more legible and trustworthy to human observers. I study whether frontier vision-language models can serve as automated proxies for human judgment of robot motion, and how these judgments can guide policy learning without human-in-the-loop annotation at scale.

Vision-Language Models as Proxies for Human Judgment of Robot Motion Legibility
Anudeep Sai Gottapu, Nakul Gopalan
MS Thesis · LOGOS Robotics Lab, Arizona State University · Committee: Hani ben Amor, Kunal Garg · Approved April 2026
We ask whether frontier VLMs (Gemini 3 Pro, GPT-5.4, Claude Opus 4.5) can replace human observers in evaluating robot motion legibility, building a cumulative prefix-frame prompting pipeline benchmarked on 8 manipulation scenarios and achieving 67.7% goal-inference accuracy with Gemini. We extend this to guide Best-of-K trajectory selection inside a behavior-conditioned diffusion policy, achieving L=0.955 legibility with CFG+VLM reranking vs. a baseline of 0.919 — showing that VLM scoring and CFG are complementary, not interchangeable.
